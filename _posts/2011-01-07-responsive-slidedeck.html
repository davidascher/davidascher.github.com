Motivation

We've been using the web for years to present slides, sometimes using baroque stacks of Flash players to display PDF slides exported from Powerpoint (gah!), sometimes building fancy video & 3D explorations of slide spaces. None of them work on phones.

I had to give a presentation on making apps for the web, and figured I'd use the occasion to promote responsive design & related technologies. I shamelessly and gratefully used Twitter's awesome Boostrap framework (using a slightly prerelease version from their responsive v2).

Demo

Try it out!

What's missing

  • Keyboard navigation
  • A new look at the IA -- I had people complain that it was hard to self-navigate
  • A real designer doing it right

Status

It did the job during the presentation (scaling down the window into a phone aspect ratio and having the presentation adapt got a big reaction), and I'm not working on it at present.

Feel free to fork it, reuse it, whatever.
